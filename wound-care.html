Minor Cuts & Wound Care

Stop bleeding, rinse well, and cover smartly.

Purpose

A quick, ship-friendly routine for small cuts, scrapes, and blisters.

Jump straight to the section you need.

Quick steps Ongoing care Avoid References
Quick Steps Immediate wound-care steps
  • Apply firm pressure with clean cloth or gauze to stop bleeding.
  • Rinse under gentle potable water for 2-5 minutes; use mild soap on nearby skin.
  • Apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly (or antibiotic ointment if visibly dirty).
  • Cover with a clean dressing and replace if wet or dirty.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Wet decks and gear friction reopen minor cuts.
  • Delayed cleaning during evolutions leaves grime in wounds.
  • Harbour, bilge, or seawater exposure raises contamination risk.
Ongoing Care Protect and monitor healing
  • Keep wounds covered and change dressings at least daily, or sooner if wet.
  • Use waterproof dressing when spray or immersion is likely.
  • Check once per watch for redness, warmth, swelling, pus, or foul odour.
  • Clean minor wounds generally heal well with petroleum jelly and clean coverage.
Tetanus check
  • Routine booster is usually every 10 years.
  • For dirty wounds, a booster may be needed sooner (often if more than 5 years).
  • Sick Bay can check your immunization status.
Things to Avoid Do not slow healing unintentionally
  • Do not pour hydrogen peroxide, alcohol, or full-strength iodine into the wound.
  • Do not scrub raw tissue or pick scabs.
  • Do not leave wounds open to seawater or dirty gloves/boots.
  • Do not close deep or gaping wounds yourself with tape/glue.
If exposed to seawater or dirty water
  • Rinse promptly with potable water and mild soap.
  • Re-apply ointment and a clean waterproof dressing.

Motion

Seasickness

Fresh air, horizon, light snacks, hydration, and safety checks to keep you on watch.

Purpose

Simple, low-risk steps to manage motion sickness and know when medical help is needed.

Try this now (60 seconds)

Immediate steps

  • Get fresh air; face the horizon. Avoid screens/reading until steadier.
  • Take small sips of water or electrolyte drink; eat a bland snack (cracker, dry toast).
  • Loosen tight gear; avoid strong smells if possible.
  • Sit where motion is smaller (midship, low) if duties allow.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Fatigue lowers your tolerance to motion.
  • Anxiety, heat, and exhaust/galley smells add nausea.
  • Working below decks increases motion and limits fresh air.

Self-care for the next 24–72 hours

Stay steady

  • Eat small, bland, frequent snacks; avoid heavy, greasy meals.
  • Keep sipping fluids; aim for urine that is pale yellow.
  • Rest when you can—fatigue worsens nausea.
  • Choose watch spots with airflow and sightlines if possible.
  • Optional low-risk extras: ginger candies/tea or acupressure wrist bands.

Avoid / common mistakes

Keep it safe

  • Skipping fluids—dehydration makes nausea worse.
  • Taking sedating medication before safety-critical duties without telling your supervisor.
  • Combining sedating meds with alcohol or other sedatives.
  • Going below decks immediately after eating if you can avoid it.
Medication safety
  • Common motion-sickness meds can cause drowsiness and blurred vision.
  • Do not mix with alcohol or other sedating drugs.
  • Tell your supervisor if you take a sedating med before duty; adjust tasks if needed.
Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Vomiting plus unable to keep fluids down or signs of dehydration (very dry mouth, dizziness, minimal urine).
  • Vomiting blood or material that looks like coffee grounds.
  • Severe abdominal pain, confusion, or fainting.
Not improving (within 24–72 hours)
  • Nausea that prevents eating or drinking for more than a day.
  • Persistent vomiting despite self-care and rest.
  • Need for medication guidance or ongoing refills.

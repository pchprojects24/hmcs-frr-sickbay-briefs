Purpose

Simple, low-risk steps to manage motion sickness and know when medical help is needed.

Try this now (60 seconds) Immediate steps to settle nausea
  • Get fresh air and face the horizon. Pause reading/screens until steadier.
  • Take small sips of water or oral rehydration/electrolyte drink.
  • Eat a bland snack (cracker, dry toast) if tolerated.
  • Loosen tight gear and reduce strong smells if possible.
  • If duties allow, stay midship and low where motion is usually reduced.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Fatigue lowers motion tolerance.
  • Anxiety, heat, and exhaust/galley smells add nausea.
  • Working below decks increases motion cues and limits fresh air.
Next 24-72 hours Stay hydrated and prevent rebound symptoms
  • Eat small, bland, frequent snacks; avoid heavy or greasy meals.
  • Keep sipping fluids; aim for pale yellow urine.
  • Rest when possible because fatigue worsens nausea.
  • Choose watch spots with airflow and horizon sightline when practical.
  • Optional low-risk extras: ginger tea/candies or acupressure wrist bands.
  • If medication is recommended by Sick Bay, take it early (before expected motion), not after severe nausea starts.
Avoid / common mistakes Keep it safe while on duty
  • Skipping fluids; dehydration worsens nausea and fatigue.
  • Taking sedating medication before safety-critical duties without informing supervisor.
  • Combining sedating medication with alcohol or other sedatives.
  • Going below decks immediately after meals if avoidable.
Medication safety
  • Common motion-sickness medicines can cause drowsiness, blurry vision, and slower reaction time.
  • Do not mix with alcohol or other sedating drugs.
  • Tell your supervisor if you have taken a sedating medicine before duty.
When to go to Sick Bay (urgent vs soon) Escalate early to prevent dehydration
Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Repeated vomiting and unable to keep fluids down.
  • Signs of dehydration: very dry mouth, dizziness, confusion, or minimal urine.
  • Vomiting blood or material that looks like coffee grounds.
  • Severe abdominal pain, fainting, or confusion.
Book soon (within 24-72 hours)
  • Nausea limiting eating/drinking for more than a day.
  • Persistent vomiting despite rest and hydration steps.
  • You need medication guidance for recurrent episodes.

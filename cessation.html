Substance Use & Addiction Support

On-base and Nova Scotia resources for tobacco, vaping, alcohol, drugs, opioids, gambling, and general addiction support — confidential and available to all CAF members.

In crisis right now?

Call or text 988 (national crisis line, 24/7) — or call 1-800-268-7708 (CFMAP, 24/7). For medical emergencies call 911.

About These Resources

Support is available for a wide range of substance use and addictions concerns — not just tobacco. All CAF programs are confidential. Provincial programs may require a Nova Scotia Health Card. Self-referral is welcome for most services.

⚕ On-Base CAF Support

CAF members at Stadacona have direct access to both pharmacy and clinical addiction services — no referral required for most programs.

Addiction Prevention & Treatment — Stadacona

Assessment, individual counselling, group programs, and treatment planning for any substance use concern, including alcohol, drugs, and tobacco.

Phone: 902-721-8600
Hours: Monday to Friday, 0730–1530

Pharmacy — Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT)

Visit the Stadacona base clinic pharmacy for patches, gum, and other NRT products at no cost to CAF members.

Phone: 721-8757
Hours: Monday to Friday, 0730–1530 (closed Thursday 1300–1400)

Canadian Forces Member Assistance Program (CFMAP)

Confidential counselling and referral for substance use, stress, family concerns, and psychological well-being — available to CAF members and their families.

  • Call: 1-800-268-7708 — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
  • Covers alcohol, drug use, and co-occurring mental health concerns
  • Can facilitate referrals to community treatment providers

🚭 Tobacco & Vaping Cessation

Free and low-cost quit programs available provincially and through community pharmacies.

Tobacco Free Nova Scotia — Quitline

Quitline counselling, secure chat, SMS support, quit packs, and multilingual resources.

  • Call the Quitline: 811
  • Text SMOKEFREE to +1 902-700-7700
  • Website: tobaccofree.novascotia.ca
NSHA — Stop Smoking Program

Group and individual counselling, virtual options, and access to quit aids through Nova Scotia Health Authority.

mha.nshealth.ca/en/stop-smoking-program

Smoke-Free Nova Scotia

Digital self-help tools, counselling referrals, and quit planning resources.

smokefreens.ca

PANS — Community Pharmacy Cessation Clinics (NS Health Card required)

Free smoking and vaping cessation services — counselling, quit plans, and prescriptions — at participating pharmacies across Nova Scotia.

  • Online booking: pans.ns.ca/quit-or-reduce-smoking-nicotine-products
  • Phone PANS: +1 902-422-9583

🍺 Alcohol Use Support

Support is available whether you are concerned about your own drinking, a colleague's, or a family member's.

NSHA — Addiction Services

Nova Scotia Health Authority provides community-based addiction services including assessment, individual counselling, group therapy, and withdrawal management referrals.

  • Access via 811 or your family doctor for a referral
  • Self-referral accepted at most sites
  • Website: mha.nshealth.ca
Alcoholics Anonymous — Halifax Intergroup

Free peer support meetings for people seeking recovery from alcohol use. No cost, no referral, open to anyone. Meetings held across Halifax daily.

  • Phone: 902-461-1119
  • Website: aahalifax.org
SMART Recovery

A science-based, secular alternative to 12-step programs. Focuses on self-management tools and cognitive strategies. Free online and in-person meetings available.

Website: smartrecovery.org

On-base option

CAF members can also contact Stadacona Addiction Prevention & Treatment directly for alcohol-related concerns: 902-721-8600

💊 Drugs & Opioid Safety

Harm reduction, treatment, and overdose prevention resources available in Nova Scotia.

Take Home Naloxone (THN) Program

Naloxone reverses opioid overdose. Free naloxone kits are available at most pharmacies across Nova Scotia — no prescription required. Training on how to use it takes only a few minutes.

  • Pick up a kit at any participating NS pharmacy
  • Ask the pharmacist for a Take Home Naloxone kit
  • Website: novascotia.ca/dhw/naloxone
Narcotics Anonymous — Nova Scotia

Peer support meetings for people seeking recovery from drug use. Free, confidential, open to all. Meetings across Halifax and surrounding areas.

  • Find local meetings: na.org
  • NS meeting schedule: nans.na.org
NSHA — Addiction & Withdrawal Services

Clinical support for substance use including assessment, counselling, medical withdrawal management, and referral to residential treatment.

  • Referral through 811 or your doctor
  • Self-referral accepted at most community sites
  • Website: mha.nshealth.ca
On-base option

Contact Stadacona Addiction Prevention & Treatment for any drug use concerns — fully confidential: 902-721-8600

🎲 Problem Gambling

Gambling problems are a recognized health issue. Free, confidential support is available provincially and nationally.

Nova Scotia Gambling Support Network

Free, confidential support for people affected by gambling problems — including family members. Counselling, referrals, and self-exclusion program assistance.

  • Problem Gambling Help Line: 1-888-347-8888 — available 24 hours a day
  • Website: responsiblegambling.novascotia.ca
Gamblers Anonymous

Peer support for people with gambling problems. Free, confidential meetings in Halifax and other NS communities.

Find meetings: gamblersanonymous.org

CFMAP also covers gambling concerns

Problem gambling is covered under CFMAP — call 1-800-268-7708 (24/7) for confidential support and referral.

🏥 Provincial & Community Services

General addiction and mental health referral lines serving all Nova Scotia residents.

NS Mental Health & Addictions Crisis Line

Provincial line for mental health and addictions information, crisis support, and referrals — available around the clock.

  • Phone: 1-888-429-8167 — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
811 — Nova Scotia Health Line

Speak to a registered nurse any time for health advice and referrals, including referrals to local addiction and mental health services.

  • Call: 811 — available 24 hours a day, free from any phone in NS
Canadian Mental Health Association — Nova Scotia

Community mental health and addiction programs, peer support, and public education. Offices in Halifax and across the province.

  • Website: cmhans.org
  • Phone: 902-466-6600
NSHA — Mental Health & Addictions (Full Program Directory)

Access the full directory of NSHA addiction services including outpatient, day programs, withdrawal management, and residential treatment across Nova Scotia.

Website: mha.nshealth.ca

Halifax Health Promotion Services

Not sure where to start?

Call 811 anytime — a registered nurse can point you to the right addiction or mental health service in Nova Scotia. For on-base support, call 902-721-8600 (Stadacona Addiction Prevention & Treatment, weekdays 0730–1530).

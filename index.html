When to get help

Red flags to report

Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Fever with worsening or spreading redness.
  • Rapidly increasing pain, swelling, or numbness.
  • Bleeding that will not stop with pressure.
  • Vomiting plus unable to keep fluids down.
  • Severe dizziness, confusion, or fainting.
Not improving (within 24–72 hours)
  • Symptoms not improving after good self-care.
  • Redness, pus, foul smell, or warmth around a wound or rash.
  • Repeated nosebleeds, nose/sinus blockage, or new breathing trouble.
  • Blisters, cracks, or sores that make duty or PPE difficult.
  • Any doubt about safe watchstanding—come to Sick Bay.

For emergencies, follow shipboard call/pipes procedures immediately.

General info only; not a substitute for assessment. If unsure, come to Sick Bay.

⚠️ Scabies Advisory — Active on Board

Posted: 23 March 2026  •  Status: Active

A confirmed case of scabies has been identified on board HMCS Frédérick Rolette. Scabies spreads easily in close-quarter environments. All crew members should review the information below and report any symptoms to sickbay promptly.

What Is Scabies?

Scabies is a contagious skin infestation caused by a microscopic mite (Sarcoptes scabiei). The mites burrow into the outer layer of skin, causing intense itching and a rash. It is not a sign of poor hygiene — it spreads through prolonged direct skin contact and, less commonly, through shared bedding or clothing.

Symptoms to Watch For

  • Intense itching, especially at night or after a hot shower
  • A pimple-like rash, small blisters, or red bumps
  • Thin, irregular burrow lines on the skin (often visible in skin folds)
  • Common sites: between fingers, wrists, elbows, waistline, buttocks, and groin

Symptoms may take 2–6 weeks to appear after first exposure. If you have been in close contact with a known case, you may develop symptoms sooner (1–4 days).

What You Should Do

  • Report to sickbay immediately if you have any of the above symptoms.
  • Do not self-treat — prescription medication (permethrin cream) is required and is available through sickbay.
  • All close contacts must be assessed and treated at the same time, even without symptoms, to prevent reinfection.
  • Wash all worn clothing, bedding, and towels in hot water (≥60°C) and dry on high heat. Items that cannot be washed should be sealed in a bag for at least 72 hours.
  • Avoid sharing towels, clothing, or bedding until cleared by medical staff.

Treatment

Treatment is effective. Permethrin 5% cream is applied to the entire body from neck to toes, left on overnight, then washed off. A second application one week later is usually recommended. Itching may continue for 1–2 weeks after treatment — this is normal and does not mean treatment has failed.

Report to Sickbay

If you have symptoms or have had prolonged skin-to-skin contact with someone who may have scabies, come to sickbay during sick parade or contact medical staff directly. Early treatment protects you and the rest of the crew.

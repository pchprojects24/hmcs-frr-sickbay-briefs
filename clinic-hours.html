Mental Health Services consists of four programs offering treatment and counselling services aimed at assisting military personnel and/or their families with mental health, social, and spiritual concerns.

Psychosocial Services is a direct-access service for counselling and support.

The following three programs provide secondary-level medical treatment: Operational Trauma & Stress Support Centre, Addiction Prevention & Treatment Program, and General Mental Health.

Mental Health Services staff also provide education and information sessions on general mental health concerns and available services.

Mental health program Addiction Prevention & Treatment Program
Phone: 721-8600
  • Provides full assessment, intensive group treatment, individual counselling, psychotherapy, and outreach presentations.
  • Services are provided to military personnel and RCMP.
  • Educational components may involve families and supervisors.
  • Treatment options range from individual counselling to an intensive day-treatment program.
Mental health program General Mental Health
Phone: 721-8012
  • Assists military personnel with a variety of mental health issues not directly related to operational or workplace experience.
  • Services include assessment, education, counselling, psychotherapy, monitoring, and follow-up.
  • Referrals are accepted from physicians or other providers within Mental Health Services and are triaged for urgency and required expertise.
Mental health program Operational Trauma & Stress Support Centre
Phone: 721-5703
  • Provides assessment and treatment for serving members and veterans with mental disorders secondary to operational or occupational exposure, including PTSD.
  • Spouses/partners may participate in educational components of treatment.
  • For serving military personnel, referral through one’s physician is preferred.
  • Referrals from chaplains and other Mental Health Services providers are also accepted, with subsequent medical consultation.
  • For veterans, referral by the regional medical officer for Veterans Affairs Canada is required.
Mental health program Psychosocial Services
Phone: 721-8607
  • Provides individual, couple, and family counselling.
  • Provides suitability screenings, compassionate assessments, addiction screening, and assessment.
  • Team includes social workers, mental health nurses, and addiction counsellors.
  • Group psycho-educational programs and presentations may also be available.
  • Referrals may be self-initiated or made through physicians, chaplains, other Mental Health Services providers, MFRC staff, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants.
  • After-hours appointments are available.
Mental health program Spiritual Counselling / Care
Phone: 721-8607
  • Offered by a military chaplain with post-secondary training in pastoral counselling.
  • Supports members with issues related to spirituality, hope, spiritual growth, healing, forgiveness, and meaning-making.
  • Counselling is not specifically related to religion.
  • Self-referrals are welcome, as are referrals from other health care providers.

Fasting information

Fasting means you should not have anything to eat or drink the night before blood collection.

  • Water is allowed
  • No alcohol for 72 hours prior to visit
  • Glucose, cholesterol, triglyceride, HDL: 10 to 12 hour fasting
  • Do not fast longer than 14 hours
  • For fasting blood work: report to blood collection services prior to 0930 hrs
  • Do not stop taking medications unless advised by your physician

Frequently asked questions about fasting

  • May I drink water? Yes, but no flavoured water
  • Should I take my medications? Yes, unless your doctor tells you not to
  • May I drink juice, coffee, tea, or milk? No
  • May I chew gum? No
  • May I have a cough drop or mint? No
  • May I smoke? No
  • May I do my exercise routine? No

24/7 support Canadian Forces Member Assistance Program (CFMAP)

CFMAP is a DND-sponsored employee assistance service that provides confidential assistance 24/7 in areas such as:

  • Marital and family problems
  • Work-related problems
  • Emotional and psychological problems
  • Financial difficulties

The program includes support for victims of sexual assault and harassment and counselling services addressing stresses at home and in the workplace.

1-800-268-7708

Mental Health Services overview

Mental Health Services consists of four programs offering treatment and counselling for military personnel and/or their families. Psychosocial Services is direct-access for counselling and support; the remaining programs provide secondary-level medical treatment.

  • Operational Trauma & Stress Support Centre
  • Addiction Prevention & Treatment Program
  • General Mental Health
  • Psychosocial Services

Mental Health Services staff also provide education and information sessions on general mental health concerns and available services.

Program details

Addiction Prevention & Treatment Program (902-721-8600)
  • Full assessment, intensive group treatment, individual counselling, psychotherapy, and outreach presentations.
  • Services for military personnel and RCMP; education may involve families and supervisors.
  • Treatment ranges from individual counselling to intensive day-treatment.
General Mental Health (902-721-8012)
  • Assists with a variety of mental health issues not directly related to operational or workplace experience.
  • Assessment, education, counselling, psychotherapy, monitoring, and follow-up.
  • Referrals accepted from physicians or other providers within Mental Health Services; triaged for urgency and expertise.
Operational Trauma & Stress Support Centre (902-721-5703)
  • Assessment and treatment for serving members and veterans with mental disorders secondary to operational or occupational exposure (including PTSD).
  • Spouses/partners may participate in educational components.
  • Preferred referral: through one’s physician (serving members) or regional medical officer for Veterans Affairs Canada (veterans); referrals from chaplains/other providers also accepted with medical consultation.
Psychosocial Services (902-721-8607)
  • Direct-access individual, couple, and family counselling; suitability screenings, compassionate assessments, addiction screening, and assessment.
  • Team includes social workers, mental health nurses, and addiction counsellors; group psycho-education may be available.
  • Self-referrals and referrals from physicians, chaplains, MFRC staff, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants accepted; after-hours appointments available.
Spiritual Counselling/Care (902-721-8607)
  • Provided by a military chaplain with training in pastoral counselling.
  • Supports issues related to spirituality, hope, growth, healing, forgiveness, and meaning-making (not religion-specific).
  • Self-referrals and referrals from other health care providers are welcome.

Fasting information

Fasting means you should not eat or drink the night before blood collection.

  • Water is allowed
  • No alcohol for 72 hours prior to visit
  • Glucose, cholesterol, triglyceride, HDL: 10 to 12 hour fasting
  • Do not fast longer than 14 hours
  • For fasting blood work: report to blood collection services prior to 0930 hrs

Do not stop taking medications unless advised by your physician.

Frequently asked questions about fasting

  • May I drink water? Yes, but no flavoured water
  • Should I take my medications? Yes, unless your doctor tells you not to
  • May I drink juice, coffee, tea, or milk? No
  • May I chew gum? No
  • May I have a cough drop or mint? No
  • May I smoke? No
  • May I do my exercise routine? No

Shaving & Razor Bumps

Reduce burn and bumps, keep blades clean, and spot infection early.

Purpose

Make shipboard shaving safer and cleaner with quick prep and simple aftercare.

Try this now (60 seconds) Immediate steps
  • Take a warm shower or press a warm cloth on your face/neck to soften hair.
  • Shave with the grain, using light pressure and one slow pass.
  • Rinse the blade under hot water between strokes.
  • Rinse skin with cool water and apply a gentle, fragrance-free moisturizer.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Helmet straps and collars rub freshly shaved skin.
  • Sweat and salt sit on skin when showers are delayed.
  • Limited time to clean or swap blades on exercise days.
Self-care for the next 24–72 hours Keep it clean and calm
  • Use a clean, sharp razor; rinse and dry it after use. Replace cartridges regularly.
  • If you can, give irritated skin a 1–2 day break or use an electric trimmer on a higher setting.
  • Shave with the grain only; avoid repeated passes on the same spot.
  • Apply a light, non-scented moisturizer after shaving. Stop any over-the-counter product if it burns or peels.
  • Keep straps, collars, and chin pieces clean to reduce friction grime.
Avoid / common mistakes Protect skin
  • Dry shaving or shaving without softening hair first.
  • Pressing hard, doing multiple fast passes, or going against the grain.
  • Sharing razors or trimmers—easy way to spread infection.
  • Using dull blades or letting them rust in a damp head.
Irritation vs infection
  • Irritation: burning or redness right after shaving that settles in hours.
  • Possible infection: pus or crusting in bumps, spreading redness, or pain that is increasing after a day or two.

Stop shaving over infected bumps; keep area clean and let Sick Bay assess.

Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Rapidly spreading redness, streaks, or swelling.
  • Fever, chills, or feeling unwell with facial swelling.
  • Painful, tight skin around the jaw or neck.
Not improving (within 24–72 hours)
  • Bumps with pus or crusting after 2–3 days of gentle care.
  • Repeated ingrown hairs despite shaving with the grain.
  • Razor burn that keeps you from wearing PPE or straps comfortably.
More info (optional) Canadian resources
  • Canadian Dermatology Association: folliculitis overview

In Nova Scotia, you can also call 811 for nurse advice (when ashore/when available).

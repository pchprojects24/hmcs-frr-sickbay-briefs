Shaving & Razor Bumps

Reduce burn and bumps, keep blades clean, and spot infection early.

Purpose

Make shipboard shaving safer and cleaner with quick prep and simple aftercare.

Jump straight to the section you need.

Try now Next 24-72h Avoid When to go Resources
Try this now (60 seconds) Immediate steps to lower razor irritation
  • Warm the skin first with a shower or warm wet cloth.
  • Shave with the grain, light pressure, and a single slow pass.
  • Rinse blade between strokes and stop if skin starts burning.
  • Rinse with cool water and apply a gentle fragrance-free moisturizer.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Helmet straps and collars rub freshly shaved skin.
  • Sweat and salt stay on skin when showers are delayed.
  • Busy routines can mean delayed blade cleaning/replacement.
Next 24-72 hours Keep skin calm and reduce ingrown hairs
  • Use a clean, sharp razor; rinse and dry it after use.
  • If irritated, take a 1-2 day shaving break or use an electric trimmer with a higher guard.
  • Continue shaving with the grain only; avoid repeated passes.
  • Apply a light, non-scented moisturizer after shaving. Stop products that sting or peel.
  • Keep straps, collars, and chin pieces clean to reduce friction and grime.
  • If you frequently get ingrown hairs, avoid very close shaving and consider single-blade options or guarded trimmers.
Avoid / common mistakes Prevent irritation from becoming infection
  • Dry shaving or skipping hair-softening prep.
  • Pressing hard, shaving against the grain, or doing repeated rapid passes.
  • Sharing razors or trimmers.
  • Using dull blades or storing razors wet in closed spaces.
  • Picking or squeezing razor bumps.
Irritation vs possible infection
  • Irritation: burning/redness right after shaving that settles within hours.
  • Possible infection: pus/crusting, spreading redness, or pain increasing after 1-2 days.

Stop shaving over infected bumps; keep area clean and let Sick Bay assess.

When to go to Sick Bay (urgent vs soon) Know your escalation points
Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Rapidly spreading redness, streaks, or swelling.
  • Fever, chills, or feeling unwell with facial swelling.
  • Painful, tight skin around jaw/neck, or trouble opening mouth.
Book soon (within 24-72 hours)
  • Bumps with pus or crusting after 2-3 days of gentle care.
  • Repeated ingrown hairs despite shaving with the grain.
  • Razor burn that prevents comfortable PPE use.

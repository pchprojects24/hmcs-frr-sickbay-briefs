What‚Äôs going on with my skin?

Razor burn (irritation)

  • Red, burning or stinging skin. Caused by friction, dull blades, or pressing too hard.

Razor bumps (Ingrown hairs)

  • Small firm bumps, often with a hair trapped inside. Common on curly/coarse hair.

Folliculitis (Infected follicles)

  • Bacteria get into cut follicles. Small sore pimples with white/yellow heads.

ü™í Shaving Gear

Multi-blade cartridges

  • Pros: fast, very close shave.
  • Cons: more friction; higher risk of ingrown hairs.
  • Tip: Use very light pressure.

Single-blade (Safety) Razors

  • Pros: cuts at skin level; better for bump-prone skin.
  • Cons: learning curve required.

Electric shavers

  • Pros: leaves tiny stubble (fewer ingrowns); quick.
  • Cons: not baby-smooth; requires power/cleaning.

Depilatories (Creams)

  • Pros: no blade; hair regrows blunt.
  • Cons: can burn skin; strong smell. Must follow directions exactly.
üß¥ Self-Care Kit

Things to keep in your wash bag:

  • Lubricant: sensitive skin shaving cream or gel.
  • Moisturizer: alcohol-free balm.

Optional Extras

  • Mild chemical exfoliant: (salicylic acid) a few times a week to prevent trapped hairs.
  • Benzoyl peroxide wash: once daily if you get pimple-like folliculitis.
  • Hydrocortisone cream (OTC): for short-term use on bad razor burn (few days max).

Start new products slowly and one at a time.

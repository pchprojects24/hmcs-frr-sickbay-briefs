Foot Care at Sea

Dry feet, early hotspot care, and quick action to avoid blisters or infection.

Purpose

Keep feet comfortable and safe for long watches in damp boots.

Jump straight to the section you need.

Quick steps Ongoing care Avoid References
Quick Steps Immediate reset for wet or sore feet
  • Change into dry socks and dry carefully between toes.
  • Air boots and insoles away from direct heat.
  • Check for hotspots and protect them before they blister.
  • Gently dry any macerated (whitish, soft) skin.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Long watches in damp boots with limited drying time.
  • Sweat plus cold-wet exposure soften skin and increase breakdown.
  • Gear friction and ladders can turn hotspots into blisters quickly.
Ongoing Care Protect skin, nails, and toe-web spaces
  • Rotate socks through drying; use liner socks if you blister easily.
  • Apply petroleum jelly or blister tape to hotspots before watch.
  • If spray/immersion is likely, use a waterproof dressing over clean skin and change it once dry.
  • Keep toe webs dry; remove boots when safe to air feet.
  • Trim toenails straight across and not too short to reduce ingrown nails.
Hotspot to blister micro-protocol
  • Stop, dry the area, and change socks.
  • Pad with moleskin/blister dressing and avoid wrinkles.
  • Re-check halfway through the next watch.
Athlete's foot basics
  • Keep toe webs dry and change socks often.
  • Use an over-the-counter antifungal cream as directed, and continue a few days after rash clears.
  • Do not share shower shoes, towels, or nail tools.
Things to Avoid Small issues can escalate quickly
  • Ignoring hotspots instead of treating them early.
  • Staying in wet socks for an entire watch.
  • Using tight tape or wraps that reduce circulation.
  • Popping blisters with unclean tools.
Immersion foot warning

Prolonged cold-wet exposure with numb, tingling, pale, or mottled skin needs prompt assessment.

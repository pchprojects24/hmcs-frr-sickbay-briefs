Why this matters at sea

Cold, dry outside air + heated ship‚Äôs air = very low humidity. That dry air pulls water out of your skin, lips and nose.

Result: dry, itchy hands, cracked heels, chapped lips and more nosebleeds than usual. A bit of daily self-care and a few cheap items from shore can prevent most of it.

üßäWhat cold, dry air does

In winter and during cold-weather trials:

  • Cold air holds very little moisture. Heating it inside the ship dries it out even more.
  • Dry air pulls water from your skin and the lining of your nose, so they dry out and can crack.
  • Wind, salt spray, frequent hand washing, gloves and long hot showers all make the drying worse.

  • Dry, tight, itchy skin on hands, arms or legs.
  • Cracked knuckles or fingertips that sting or bleed.
  • Chapped, split lips; sore corners of the mouth.
  • ‚ÄúBeard dandruff‚Äù or an itchy, flaky scalp.
  • Dry, cracked heels.
  • Dry, crusty nose and more nosebleeds than usual.
  • Scratchy, dry throat after watch or overnight.
  • Eczema patches (if you‚Äôve had eczema before) flaring in winter.

üß∞Stuff worth buying for yourself

Before winter or cold-weather trials, consider picking up:

  • 1 thick, fragrance-free moisturizing cream (tub or tube ‚Äì not a thin lotion).
  • 1 unscented lip balm or small tub of petroleum jelly.
  • 1 small tube of hand cream you actually like using.
  • 1 bottle of saline nasal spray.
  • Optional: a foot/heel cream, thin cotton gloves or socks for overnight use with cream.
Small, simple items like these prevent most winter skin issues and keep you from fighting cracked hands and lips all deployment.

üëêHands

Day-to-day

  • Use warm, not hot water when you can.
  • If there‚Äôs a choice, pick the gentlest soap or hand wash.
  • After every wash, put on a small amount of hand cream. Keep a tube in your pocket.

At night

  • Put a thick layer of cream or petroleum jelly on the backs of your hands and over any cracks.
  • If you have them, wear thin cotton gloves or clean socks over your hands for a few hours or overnight.

On duty / cleaning

  • Wear protective gloves for cleaning chemicals and detergents.
  • If a particular product always irritates your skin, try to avoid direct contact or use extra protection.

  • Carry a plain lip balm or petroleum jelly and use it often:

    • every 1‚Äì2 hours in cold wind,
    • before sleep.
  • Don‚Äôt lick your lips ‚Äì saliva evaporates and dries them more.

üß¥Skin on body and face

Showers

  • Keep showers short and warm, not long and hot.
  • Use soap mainly on armpits, groin and feet. Rinse the rest rather than scrubbing hard.

After shower

  • Pat skin dry, then while it‚Äôs still slightly damp, put cream on arms, legs, hands and any dry areas.
  • For very dry or cracked spots, use a thin layer of petroleum jelly over cream at night.

Out on deck

  • Cover as much skin as possible: hat, neck gaiter/face covering, gloves.
  • If wool itches, wear a cotton T-shirt or base layer under wool.

  • Before bed:

üßîScalp and beard

  • For beards: wash, rinse well, then use a simple beard oil or conditioner if you like to keep skin and hair from drying out.
  • Wear a hat in the cold; if wool hats itch, use one with a liner or wear a thin layer underneath.

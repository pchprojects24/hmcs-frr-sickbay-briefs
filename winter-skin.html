Cold Weather Skin

Keep hands, face, lips, and nose from cracking in cold, dry air. Know when it is more than dryness.

Purpose

Simple routines to protect skin and nose on cold, windy sails and in dry, heated compartments.

Jump straight to what you need.

Try this now (60 seconds) Immediate steps for cold-weather dryness
  • Rub a plain, fragrance-free cream or ointment into hands and face now; keep a travel tube in your pocket.
  • Put lip balm on and pocket it; reapply every 1-2 hours in wind and before sleep.
  • Swap out damp gloves or socks; air liners and mitts near (not on) heat.
  • Use saline spray once in each nostril; apply a tiny dab of petroleum jelly just inside the rim if raw.
  • Drink water now; dehydration makes dryness worse.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Cold wind and salt spray strip skin oils quickly.
  • Forced-air heat on board lowers humidity.
  • Frequent sanitizer and wet work dry hands and cause cracks.
  • Gear friction (collars, balaclavas, gloves) rubs already dry skin.

Rule of thumb: ointment > cream > lotion for staying power.

Next 24-72 hours Keep moisture in and prevent repeat cracking
  • After each wash or sanitizer use, apply a pea-sized amount of plain cream or ointment to hands and knuckles.
  • Take short, warm (not hot) showers; pat dry and moisturize while skin is still slightly damp.
  • Before sleep, use a thicker layer on hands, heels, and lips; add clean cotton gloves or socks if available.
  • On deck, cover cheeks, nose, and ears; use liners if wool gear irritates skin.
  • Change out of damp socks quickly; moisturize cracked heels at night.
  • If you are in sun plus snow/water glare, use broad-spectrum SPF 30+ on exposed skin.
Nosebleed steps
  • Sit up, lean forward, and pinch the soft part of your nose firmly for 10-15 minutes without peeking.
  • If still bleeding, repeat once more for 10-15 minutes. Spit blood out; do not tilt your head back.
  • Seek help if bleeding continues after two rounds of pressure or if you feel lightheaded.
Avoid / common mistakes Protect the skin barrier
  • Long, very hot showers that strip natural oils.
  • Using only thin lotion once a day; thick products used often work better.
  • Licking lips or picking at skin cracks.
  • Overusing antiseptics on intact skin; they can worsen dryness.
  • Leaving wet gloves or socks on, or putting wet gear directly on heaters.
Not always just dry skin

Numb, hard, waxy, pale, or blue skin after cold exposure can mean cold injury. Cover gently and get help right away.

Could this be eczema? Itchy, recurring rough patches that return in the same spots may be eczema, not simple dryness. Infection red flags include spreading redness, warmth, pus, or bad smell.
When to go to Sick Bay (urgent vs soon) Know your escalation points
Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Cold exposure with numb, hard, waxy, pale, or blue skin.
  • Nosebleed that continues after two full rounds of direct pressure, or causes dizziness/faintness.
  • Rapidly spreading redness, swelling, fever, or streaking from a crack or rash.
  • Cannot feel fingertips or toes, or pain is severe.
Book soon (within 24-72 hours)
  • Cracks not healing after 2-3 days of consistent moisturizing.
  • Repeated nosebleeds, crusting inside the nose, or one-sided blockage.
  • Itchy patches that keep returning or disrupt sleep.
  • Any skin issue making PPE or watches difficult to do safely.

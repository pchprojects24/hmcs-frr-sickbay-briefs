Cold, Dry Air & Your Skin

Select a topic below to see self-care instructions.

Why this matters

Cold air + ship heating = low humidity. This pulls water from your skin. Simple items like moisturizing cream, lip balm, and saline spray will prevent most issues.

üëê Hands & Knuckles

Day-to-day

  • Use warm, not hot water when you can.
  • Use the gentlest soap available.
  • After every wash, put on a small amount of hand cream. Keep a tube in your pocket.

At night

  • Put a thick layer of cream or petroleum jelly on hands/cracks.
  • Wear thin cotton gloves or clean socks over hands overnight if available.

On duty

  • Wear protective gloves for cleaning chemicals.

üöø Face & Body (Showers)

Showers

  • Keep showers short and warm, not long and hot.
  • Use soap mainly on armpits, groin, and feet. Rinse the rest.

After shower

  • Pat skin dry. While slightly damp, put cream on arms, legs, and hands.
  • Use petroleum jelly over cream for very dry spots at night.

Out on deck

  • Cover skin: hat, neck gaiter, gloves.
  • If wool itches, wear cotton underneath.

  • Change out of wet socks immediately.
  • Before bed: rub thick cream/ointment into heels and put on clean socks.
  • Days off: soak feet in warm water for 10 mins, file hard skin, then moisturize.
  • See medical if heel cracks are deep, painful, or look infected.

Cold Weather Skin

Keep skin from cracking or flaring when sailing in Arctic or winter conditions.

Why this matters

Cold air + ship heating = very dry air inside. This pulls water from your skin, lips, and nose.

Simple items like a plain, fragrance-free moisturizing cream, lip balm, and saline nasal spray prevent most issues if you use them early and often.

Quick actions: Moisturize twice daily, especially hands/face. Keep gloves dry and change socks often.

Day-to-day

  • Use warm, not hot water when you can.
  • Use the gentlest soap available; alcohol hand rubs and harsh soaps dry skin fast.
  • After every wash or sanitizer use, rub in a small amount of plain, fragrance-free hand cream (cream or ointment works better than thin lotion). Keep a tube in your pocket.

At night

  • Put a thick layer of cream or petroleum jelly on dry areas and skin cracks.
  • Wear thin cotton gloves or clean socks over hands overnight if available.

On duty

  • Wear protective gloves for cleaning chemicals or frequent wet work.

  • If corners of the mouth crack, keep them clean and greasy with ointment; see medical if they are very sore or crusting.
üöøShowers and Skin Protection

Showers

  • Keep showers short and warm, not long and hot.
  • Use soap mainly on armpits, groin, and feet. Rinse the rest.

After shower

  • Pat skin dry. While slightly damp, put a plain, fragrance-free cream on arms, legs, and hands (cream/ointment works better than lotion).
  • Use petroleum jelly over cream for very dry spots at night (knuckles, shins, heels).

Out on deck

  • Cover skin: hat, neck gaiter, gloves; cover cheeks and nose in cold wind.
  • If wool itches, wear cotton underneath to reduce rubbing.
üë£Foot Care in Winter
  • Change out of wet or very damp socks as soon as you can.
  • Before bed: rub thick cream/ointment into heels and dry, cracked areas, then put on clean socks.
  • Days off: soak feet in warm water for 10 mins, gently file hard skin, then moisturize.
  • See medical if heel cracks are deep, painful, look infected, or make it hard to stand watches.

  • Use gentle shampoo (daily if oily, every 2‚Äì3 days if scalp is dry/itchy).
  • Use anti-dandruff shampoo if you get flakes; leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing.

  • Wear a hat liner if wool hats make you itch.
  • See medical if you have a red, very itchy rash or flaking that doesn't settle with over-the-counter shampoo.

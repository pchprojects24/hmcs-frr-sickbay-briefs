Skin / Prevention

Cold Weather Skin

Keep hands, face, lips, and nose from cracking in cold, dry air. Know when it is more than dryness.

Purpose

Simple routines to protect skin and nose on cold, windy sails and in dry, heated compartments.

Try this now (60 seconds)

Immediate steps

  • Rub a plain, fragrance-free cream or ointment into hands and face now; keep a travel tube in your pocket.
  • Put lip balm on and pocket it; reapply every 1–2 hours in wind and before sleep.
  • Swap out any damp gloves or socks; air liners and mitts near (not on) heat.
  • Use saline spray once in each nostril; apply a tiny dab of petroleum jelly just inside the rim if raw.
  • Drink some water; dryness worsens with dehydration.
Why this is worse at sea
  • Cold wind and salt spray strip skin oils quickly.
  • Forced-air heat on board lowers humidity.
  • Frequent sanitizer and wet work dry hands and cause cracks.
  • Gear friction (collars, balaclavas, gloves) rubs already dry skin.
Rule of thumb: ointment > cream > lotion for staying power.

Self-care for the next 24–72 hours

Keep moisture in

  • After every wash or sanitizer use, apply a pea-sized amount of plain cream/ointment to hands and knuckles.
  • Short, warm (not hot) showers; pat dry and moisturize while skin is still slightly damp.
  • Before bed: thick layer of cream or petroleum jelly on dry areas (hands, heels, lips); clean cotton gloves or socks on if available.
  • On deck: cover cheeks, nose, and ears; use liners if wool gear itches.
  • Feet: change out of damp socks quickly; grease cracked heels at night and wear clean socks.
  • Nose: saline spray several times daily; tiny dab of petroleum jelly at the front of the nostrils at night (don’t push deep).

Nosebleeds

Stop the bleed safely

  • Sit up, lean forward, and pinch the soft part of your nose firmly for 10–15 minutes without peeking.
  • If still bleeding, repeat once more for 10–15 minutes. Spit blood out; do not tilt your head back.
  • Seek help if bleeding continues after two rounds of pressure or if you feel lightheaded.

Avoid / common mistakes

Protect the barrier

  • Long, very hot showers that strip oils.
  • Using only thin lotion once a day—use thicker products more often.
  • Licking lips or picking at skin cracks.
  • Overusing antiseptics on intact skin; they worsen dryness.
  • Leaving wet gloves/socks on or underheated gear directly on heaters.
Not just dry skin

Numb, hard, waxy, pale, or blue skin after cold exposure could be cold injury. Cover gently and get help right away.

Is it eczema? Itchy, recurring rough patches that return in the same spots may be eczema, not simple dryness. Infection red flags: spreading redness, warmth, pus, or a bad smell—see Sick Bay.
Go now (urgent / same watch)
  • Cold exposure with numb, hard, waxy, pale, or blue skin.
  • Nosebleed that keeps going after two full rounds of pressure or makes you feel faint.
  • Rapidly spreading redness, swelling, fever, or streaks from a crack or rash.
  • Cannot feel fingertips or toes, or pain is severe.
Not improving (within 24–72 hours)
  • Cracks not healing after 2–3 days of good moisturizing.
  • Repeated nosebleeds, crusting inside the nose, or blocked breathing through one side.
  • Itchy patches that keep returning or disturb sleep.
  • Any skin issue making PPE/watches hard to do safely.

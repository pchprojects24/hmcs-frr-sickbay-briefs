When to Report to Sick Bay

Know when to seek medical attention and what conditions require immediate care.

Purpose

Clear guidance on when to self-manage and when to report for medical assessment.

Always Report Immediately For:
  • Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or heart palpitations
  • Loss of consciousness or confusion
  • Severe injury, uncontrolled bleeding, or suspected fracture
  • Sudden vision changes or severe headache
  • Signs of heat injury or hypothermia
  • Allergic reactions with swelling, difficulty breathing, or hives
  • Any concern about operational safety or fitness for duty

Report Same Day or Next Available Sick Call For:

  • Fever over 38.5°C (101°F) or fever lasting more than 2 days
  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea with signs of dehydration
  • Wound showing signs of infection (increased redness, warmth, pus, red streaks)
  • Pain that interferes with work, watch, or sleep
  • Symptoms lasting longer than expected or getting worse instead of better
  • New or unusual symptoms you cannot explain
  • Concerns about prescribed medications or side effects

Self-Care First, Report If No Improvement:

  • Minor cuts, scrapes, or abrasions (report if signs of infection develop)
  • Mild cold or flu symptoms without fever (report if worsening after 3-4 days)
  • Minor muscle strains or sprains (report if severe pain or no improvement in 48 hours)
  • Mild headache, minor skin irritation, or seasonal allergies (report if persistent or worsening)
When in Doubt

If you are unsure whether you should report, it is always better to check in. Medical staff would rather assess you and find nothing serious than miss something important.
